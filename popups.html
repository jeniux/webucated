Why Were Pop-Ups So Bad?

  • Websites could open unlimited pop-up windows
  • Pop-ups could spawn more pop-ups (recursive nightmare)
  • Some pop-ups tried to hide the close button
  • Others would move when you tried to close them
  • Many disguised ads as system warnings
  • Closing one pop-up would open three more
  • Your browser would crash from too many windows
  • Pop-unders would hide behind your browser

What Fixed It?

In 2004, Firefox introduced the first effective pop-up blocker. Internet Explorer added one in IE6 SP2. Safari and Chrome followed. Today, pop-up blockers are so effective that many younger people have never experienced pop-up hell. Modern browsers also restrict JavaScript's ability to open windows without user interaction.
