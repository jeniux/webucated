üì± The Skeuomorphic Era (2007-2013)

Skeuomorphism dominated Apple's iOS design from the first iPhone through iOS 6. The philosophy was that digital interfaces should mimic real-world materials to make them more familiar and approachable.

Examples from iOS 6:

  • Notes app looked like a yellow legal pad with torn paper
  • Calendar had leather stitching at the top
  • Bookshelf had wooden shelves for iBooks
  • Voice Memos looked like a vintage microphone
  • Game Center had green felt like a poker table
  • Find My Friends had leather stitching
  • Compass looked like a real compass
  • Calculator had realistic button shadows

The Great Debate: Skeuomorphism was controversial. Supporters said it made interfaces intuitive. Critics said it was tacky and limited design. In 2013, iOS 7 launched with flat design, ending the skeuomorphic era. Jony Ive famously removed all the leather, wood, and felt textures.

Today, we use "flat design" with subtle shadows and gradients. No more fake leather!

Why Did This Exist?

In 2007, touchscreen smartphones were brand new. Apple believed people needed familiar visual metaphors to understand digital interfaces. If a button looked like a real button with texture and depth, users would know to press it.

By 2013, everyone understood touchscreens, so the realistic textures became unnecessary decoration. iOS 7 ditched it all for clean, flat design.
